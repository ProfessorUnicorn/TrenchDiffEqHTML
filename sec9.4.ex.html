34.
Suppose the equation \[ P_0(x)y^{(n)}+P_1(x)y^{(n-1)}+\cdots +P_n(x)y=F(x) {\tag A} \] is normal on an interval \((a,b)\). Let \(\{y_1,y_2,\dots ,y_n\}\) be a fundamental set of solutions of its complementary equation on \((a,b)\), let \(W\) be the Wronskian of \(\{y_1,y_2,\dots ,y_n\}\), and let \(W_j\) be the determinant obtained by deleting the last row and the \(j\)-th column of \(W\). Suppose \(x_0\) is in \((a,b)\), let \[ u_j(x)=(-1)^{(n-j)}\int _{x_0}^x{F(t)W_j(t)\over P_0(t)W(t)}\,dt, \quad 1\le j\le n, \] and define \[ y_p=u_1y_1+u_2y_2+\cdots +u_ny_n. \]
  1. Show that \(y_p\) is a solution of (A) and that \[ y_p^{(r)}=u_1y^{(r)}_1+u_2y_2^{(r)}\cdots +u_ny^{(r)}_n,\quad 1 \le r \le n-1, \] and \[ y_p^{(n)}=u_1y_1^{(n)}+u_2y_2^{(n)}+\cdots +u_ny_n^{(n)}+{F\over P_0}. \] Hint: See the derivation of the method of variation of parameters at the beginning of the section.
  2. Show that \(y_p\) is the solution of the initial value problem \[ \begin {array}{r} P_0(x)y^{(n)}+P_1(x)y^{(n-1)}+\cdots +P_n(x)y=F(x),\\ y(x_0)=0,\; y'(x_0)=0,\dots ,\quad y^{(n-1)}(x_0)=0. \end {array} \]
  3. Show that \(y_p\) can be written as \[ y_p(x)=\int _{x_0}^x G(x,t)F(t)\,dt, \] where \[ G(x,t)={1\over P_0(t)W(t)}\left |\begin {array}{cccc} y_1(t)&y_2(t)&\cdots&y_n(t)\\ y_1'(t)&y_2'(t)&\cdots&y _n'(t)\\ \vdots& \vdots& \ddots& \vdots \\ y_1^{(n-2)}(t)&y_2^{(n-2)}(t)&\cdots&y_n^{(n-2)}(t)\\ y_1(x)&y_2(x)&\cdots&y _n(x) \end {array}\right |, \] which is called the Greenâ€™s function for (A).
  4. Show that \[ {\partial ^{j}G(x,t)\over \partial x^j} ={1\over P_0(t)W(t)}\left |\begin {array}{cccc} y_1(t)&y_2(t)&\cdots&y _n(t)\\ y_1'(t)&y_2'(t)&\cdots&y_n'(t)\\ \vdots& \vdots& \ddots& \vdots \\ y_1^{(n-2)}(t)&y_2^{(n-2)}(t)&\cdots&y _n^{(n-2)}(t)\\ y_1^{(j)}(x)&y_2^{(j)}(x)&\cdots&y _n^{(j)}(x) \end {array}\right |,\quad 0\le j\le n \]
  5. Show that if \(a<t<b\) then \[ \left .{\partial ^{j}G(x,t)\over \partial x^j}\right |_{x=t}= \left \{\begin {array}{cl} 0,&1\le j\le n-2,\\ \displaystyle {1\over P_0(t)},&j=n-1. \end {array}\right . \]
  6. Show that \[ y_p^{(j)}(x)=\left \{\begin {array}{cl} \displaystyle {\int _{x_0}^x {\partial ^{j}G(x,t)\over \partial x^j}F(t)\,dt},&1\le j\le n-1,\\ \displaystyle {{F(x)\over P_0(x)}+ \int _{x_0}^x{\partial^{(n)}G(x,t)\over \partial x^n}F(t)\,dt},&j=n \end {array}\right . \]
Use the method suggested by Exercise 34 to find a particular solution in the form \(y_p=\int _{x_0}^x G(x,t)F(t)\,dt\), given the indicated fundamental set of solutions. Assume that \(x\) and \(x_0\) are in an interval on which the equation is normal.

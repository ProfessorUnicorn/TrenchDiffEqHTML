Part 5: Challenge
36.
  1. Verify that if \[ y_p=A(x)\cos \omega x+B(x)\sin \omega x \] where \(A\) and \(B\) are twice differentiable, then
    \begin {eqnarray*} \frac{dy_p}{dx}&=&(\frac{dA}{dx}+\omega B)\cos \omega x+(\frac{dB}{dx}-\omega A) \sin \omega x\mbox { and}\\ \frac{d^2y_p}{dx^2}&=&(\frac{d^2A}{dx^2}+2\omega \frac{dB}{dx}-\omega ^2A)\cos \omega x +(\frac{d^2B}{dx^2}-2\omega \frac{dA}{dx}-\omega ^2B)\sin \omega x \end {eqnarray*}
  2. Use the results of (a) to verify that
    \begin {eqnarray*} a\frac{d^2y_p}{dx^2}+b\frac{dy_p}{dx}+cy_p&=&\left [(c-a\omega ^2)A+b\omega B+2a\omega \frac{dB}{dx}+b\frac{dA}{dx}+a\frac{d^2A}{dx^2}\right ] \cos \omega x+\\ && \left [-b\omega A+(c-a\omega ^2)B-2a\omega \frac{dA}{dx}+b\frac{dB}{dx}+a\frac{d^2B}{dx^2}\right ]\sin \omega x \end {eqnarray*}
  3. Use the results of (a) to verify that \[ \frac{d^2y_p}{dx^2}+\omega ^2 y_p=(\frac{d^2A}{dx^2}+2\omega \frac{dB}{dx})\cos \omega x+ (\frac{d^2B}{dx^2}-2\omega \frac{dA}{dx})\sin \omega x\]
  4. Prove Theorem 5.5.2.
37.
Let \(a\), \(b\), \(c\), and \(\omega \) be constants, with \(a\ne 0\) and \(\omega >0\), and let \[ P(x)=p_0+p_1x+\cdots +p_kx^k \quad \mbox{ and } \quad Q(x)=q_0+q_1x+\cdots +q_kx^k\] where at least one of the coefficients \(p_k\), \(q_k\) is nonzero, so \(k\) is the larger of the degrees of \(P\) and \(Q\).
  1. Show that if \(\cos \omega x\) and \(\sin \omega x\) are not solutions of the complementary equation \[ a\frac{d^2y}{dx^2}+b\frac{dy}{dx}+cy=0\] then there are polynomials \[ A(x)=A_0+A_1x+\cdots +A_kx^k \mbox {\quad and \quad } B(x)=B_0+B_1x+\cdots +B_kx^k {\tag A} \] such that \[ \begin {array}{lcl} \quad (c-a\omega ^2)A+b\omega B+2a\omega \frac{dB}{dx}+b\frac{dA}{dx}+a\frac{d^2A}{dx^2}&=&P\phantom {.}\\ -b\omega A+(c-a\omega ^2)B-2a\omega \frac{dA}{dx}+b\frac{dB}{dx}+a\frac{d^2B}{dx^2}&=&Q \end {array} \] where \((A_k,B_k)\), \((A_{k-1},B_{k-1})\), …,\((A_0,B_0)\) can be computed successively by solving the systems \[ \begin {array}{lcl} \phantom {-}(c-a\omega ^2)A_k+b\omega B_k&=&p_k\phantom {.}\\ -b\omega A_k+(c-a\omega ^2)B_k&=&q_k \end {array} \] and, if \(1\le r\le k\), \begin {array}{lcl} \phantom {-}(c-a\omega ^2)A_{k-r}+b\omega B_{k-r}&=&p_{k-r}+\cdots \phantom {.}\\ -b\omega A_{k-r}+(c-a\omega ^2)B_{k-r}&=&q_{k-r}+\cdots \end {array} where the terms indicated by “\(\cdots \)” depend upon the previously computed coefficients with subscripts greater than \(k-r\). Conclude from this and Exercise 36(b) that \[ y_p=A(x)\cos \omega x+B(x)\sin \omega x {\tag B} \] is a particular solution of \[ a\frac{d^2y}{dx^2}+b\frac{dy}{dx}+cy=P(x)\cos \omega x+Q(x)\sin \omega x\]
  2. Conclude from Exercise 36(c) that the equation \[ a(\frac{d^2y}{dx^2}+\omega ^2 y)=P(x)\cos \omega x+Q(x)\sin \omega x {\tag C} \] does not have a solution of the form (B) with \(A\) and \(B\) as in (A). Then show that there are polynomials \[ A(x)=A_0x+A_1x^2+\cdots +A_kx^{k+1}\mbox {\quad and \quad } B(x)=B_0x+B_1x^2+\cdots +B_kx^{k+1} \] such that \[ \begin {array}{rcl} a(\frac{d^2A}{dx^2}+2\omega \frac{dB}{dx})&=&P\\ a(\frac{d^2B}{dx^2}-2\omega \frac{dA}{dx})&=&Q \end {array} \] where the pairs \((A_k,B_k)\), \((A_{k-1},B_{k-1})\), …, \((A_0,B_0)\) can be computed successively as follows:
    \begin {eqnarray*} A_k&=&-{q_k\over 2a\omega (k+1)}\\ B_k&=&\phantom {-}{p_k\over 2a\omega (k+1)} \end {eqnarray*}
    and, if \(k\ge 1\),
    \begin {eqnarray*} A_{k-j}&=&-{1\over 2\omega } \left [{q_{k-j}\over a(k-j+1)}-(k-j+2)B_{k-j+1}\right ]\\ B_{k-j}&=&\phantom {-}{1\over 2\omega } \left [{p_{k-j}\over a(k-j+1)}-(k-j+2)A_{k-j+1}\right ] \end {eqnarray*}
    for \(1\le j\le k\). Conclude that (B) with this choice of the polynomials \(A\) and \(B\) is a particular solution of (C).
38.
Show that Theorem 5.5.1 implies the next theorem: Suppose \(\omega \) is a positive number and \(P\) and \(Q\) are polynomials. Let \(k\) be the larger of the degrees of \(P\) and \(Q\). Then the equation \[ a\frac{d^2y}{dx^2}+b\frac{dy}{dx}+cy=e^{\lambda x}\left (P(x)\cos \omega x+Q(x)\sin \omega x\right ) \] has a particular solution \[ y_p=e^{\lambda x}\left (A(x)\cos \omega x+B(x)\sin \omega x\right ), {\tag A} \] where \[ A(x)=A_0+A_1x+\cdots +A_kx^k \quad \mbox{ and } \quad B(x)=B_0+B_1x+\cdots +B_kx^k\] provided that \(e^{\lambda x}\cos \omega x\) and \(e^{\lambda x}\sin \omega x\) are not solutions of the complementary equation. The equation \[ a\left [\frac{d^2y}{dx^2}-2\lambda \frac{dy}{dx}+(\lambda ^2+\omega ^2)y\right ]= e^{\lambda x}\left (P(x)\cos \omega x+Q(x)\sin \omega x\right ) \] \((\)for which \(e^{\lambda x}\cos \omega x\) and \(e^{\lambda x}\sin \omega x\) are solutions of the complementary equation\()\) has a particular solution of the form (A), where \[ A(x)=A_0x+A_1x^2+\cdots +A_kx^{k+1} \mbox {\quad and \quad } B(x)=B_0x+B_1x^2+\cdots +B_kx^{k+1}\]
39.
This exercise presents a method for evaluating the integral \[ y=\int e^{\lambda x}\left (P(x)\cos \omega x+Q(x)\sin \omega x\right )\,dx \] where \(\omega \ne 0\) and \[ P(x)=p_0+p_1x+\cdots +p_kx^k,\quad Q(x)=q_0+q_1x+\cdots +q_kx^k\]
  1. Show that \(y=e^{\lambda x}u\), where \[ \frac{du}{dx}+\lambda u=P(x)\cos \omega x+Q(x)\sin \omega x. {\tag A} \]
  2. Show that (A) has a particular solution of the form \[ u_p=A(x)\cos \omega x+B(x)\sin \omega x\] where \[ A(x)=A_0+A_1x+\cdots +A_kx^k,\quad B(x)=B_0+B_1x+\cdots +B_kx^k\] and the pairs of coefficients \((A_k,B_k)\), \((A_{k-1},B_{k-1})\), …,\((A_0,B_0)\) can be computed successively as the solutions of pairs of equations obtained by equating the coefficients of \(x^r\cos \omega x\) and \(x^r\sin \omega x\) for \(r=k\), \(k-1\), …, \(0\).
  3. Conclude that \[ \int e^{\lambda x}\left (P(x)\cos \omega x+Q(x)\sin \omega x\right )\,dx = e^{\lambda x}\left (A(x)\cos \omega x+B(x)\sin \omega x\right ) +c\] where \(c\) is a constant of integration.

43.
Experiments indicate that glucose is absorbed by the body at a rate proportional to the amount of glucose present in the bloodstream. Let \(\lambda \) denote the (positive) constant of proportionality. Now suppose glucose is injected into a patient’s bloodstream at a constant rate of \(r\) units per unit of time. Let \(G=G(t)\) be the number of units in the patient’s bloodstream at time \(t>0\). Then \[ \dot{G}=-\lambda G+r\] where the first term on the right is due to the absorption of the glucose by the patient’s body and the second term is due to the injection. Determine \(G\) for \(t>0\), given that \(G(0)=G_0\). Also, find \(\lim _{t\to \infty }G(t)\).

Suppose \(f\) is continuous on an open interval \((a,b)\) and \(\alpha \) is a constant.
  1. Derive a formula for the solution of the initial value problem \[ \frac{dy}{dx}+\alpha y=f(x),\quad y(x_0)=y_0, \, {\tag A} \] where \(x_0\) is in \((a,b)\) and \(y_0\) is an arbitrary real number.
  2. Suppose \((a,b)=(a,\infty )\), \(\alpha > 0\) and \(\displaystyle {\lim _{x\to \infty } f(x)=L}\). Show that if \(y\) is the solution of (A), then \(\displaystyle {\lim _{x\to \infty } y(x)=L/\alpha }\).
46.
Assume that all functions in this exercise are defined on a common interval \((a,b)\).
  1. Prove: If \(y_1\) and \(y_2\) are solutions of \[ \frac{dy}{dx}+p(x)y=f_1(x) \] and \[ \frac{dy}{dx}+p(x)y=f_2(x) \] respectively, and \(c_1\) and \(c_2\) are constants, then \(y=c_1y_1+c_2y_2\) is a solution of \[ \frac{dy}{dx}+p(x)y=c_1f_1(x)+c_2f_2(x)\] (This is the principle of superposition.)
  2. Use (a) to show that if \(y_1\) and \(y_2\) are solutions of the nonhomogeneous equation \[ \frac{dy}{dx}+p(x)y=f(x), \, {\tag A} \] then \(y_1-y_2\) is a solution of the homogeneous equation \[ \frac{dy}{dx}+p(x)y=0. \, {\tag B} \]
  3. Use (a) to show that if \(y_1\) is a solution of (A) and \(y_2\) is a solution of (B), then \(y_1+y_2\) is a solution of (A).

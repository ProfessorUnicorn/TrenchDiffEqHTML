24.
Suppose \(p\) and \(q\) are continuous on an open interval \((a,b)\) and let \(x_0\) be in \((a,b)\). Use Theorem  5.1.1 to show that the only solution of the IVP \[ \frac{d^2y}{dx^2}+p(x)\frac{dy}{dx}+q(x)y=0,\quad y(x_0)=0,\quad \left.\frac{dy}{dx}\right|_{x_0}=0 \] on \((a,b)\) is the trivial solution \(y\equiv 0\).
25.
Suppose \(P_0\), \(P_1\), and \(P_2\) are continuous on \((a,b)\) and let \(x_0\) be in \((a,b)\). Show that if either of the following statements is true then \(P_0(x)=0\) for some \(x\) in \((a,b)\).
  1. The IVP \[ P_0(x)\frac{d^2y}{dx^2}+P_1(x)\frac{dy}{dx}+P_2(x)y=0,\quad y(x_0)=k_0,\quad \left.\frac{dy}{dx}\right|_{x_0}=k_1 \] has more than one solution on \((a,b)\).
  2. The IVP \[ P_0(x)\frac{d^2y}{dx^2}+P_1(x)\frac{dy}{dx}+P_2(x)y=0,\quad y(x_0)=0,\quad \left.\frac{dy}{dx}\right|_{x_0}=0 \] has a nontrivial solution on \((a,b)\).
26.
Suppose \(p\) and \(q\) are continuous on \((a,b)\) and \(y_1\) and \(y_2\) are solutions of \[ \frac{d^2y}{dx^2}+p(x)\frac{dy}{dx}+q(x)y=0 {\tag A} \] on \((a,b)\). Let \[ z_1=\alpha y_1+\beta y_2\quad \mbox{ and } \quad z_2=\gamma y_1+\delta y_2\] where \(\alpha \), \(\beta \), \(\gamma \), and \(\delta \) are constants. Show that if \(\{z_1,z_2\}\) is a fundamental set of solutions of (A) on \((a,b)\) then so is \(\{y_1,y_2\}\).
27.
Suppose \(p\) and \(q\) are continuous on \((a,b)\) and \(\{y_1,y_2\}\) is a fundamental set of solutions of \[ \frac{d^2y}{dx^2}+p(x)\frac{dy}{dx}+q(x)y=0 {\tag A} \] on \((a,b)\). Let \[ z_1=\alpha y_1+\beta y_2\quad \mbox{ and } \quad z_2=\gamma y_1+\delta y_2\] where \(\alpha ,\beta ,\gamma \), and \(\delta \) are constants. Show that \(\{z_1,z_2\}\) is a fundamental set of solutions of (A) on \((a,b)\) if and only if \( \alpha \gamma -\beta \delta \ne 0\).
28.
Suppose \(y_1\) is differentiable on an interval \((a,b)\) and \(y_2=ky_1\), where \(k\) is a constant. Show that the Wronskian of \(\{y_1,y_2\}\) is identically zero on \((a,b)\).
29.
Let \[ y_1=x^3\quad \mbox { and }\quad y_2=\left \{\begin {array}{rl} x^3,&x\ge 0,\\ -x^3,&x<0.\end {array}\right. \]
  1. Show that the Wronskian of \(\{y_1,y_2\}\) is defined and identically zero on \((-\infty ,\infty )\).
  2. Suppose \(a<0<b\). Show that \(\{y_1,y_2\}\) is linearly independent on \((a,b)\).
  3. Use Exercise 25(b) to show that these results don’t contradict Theorem  5.1.5, because neither \(y_1\) nor \(y_2\) can be a solution of an equation \[ \frac{d^2y}{dx^2}+p(x)\frac{dy}{dx}+q(x)y=0 \] on \((a,b)\) if \(p\) and \(q\) are continuous on \((a,b)\).
30.
Suppose \(p\) and \(q\) are continuous on \((a,b)\) and \(\{y_1,y_2\}\) is a set of solutions of \[ \frac{d^2y}{dx^2}+p(x)\frac{dy}{dx}+q(x)y=0 \] on \((a,b)\) such that either \(y_1(x_0)=y_2(x_0)=0\) or \(\left.\frac{d^2y_1}{dx^2}\right|_{x_0}=\left.\frac{d^2y_2}{dx^2}\right|_{x_0}=0\) for some \(x_0\) in \((a,b)\). Show that \(\{y_1,y_2\}\) is linearly dependent on \((a,b)\).
31.

Show that if \(y_1(x_1)=y_1(x_2)=0\), where \(a<x_1<x_2<b\), then \(y_2(x)=0\) for some \(x\) in \((x_1,x_2)\). Hint: Show that if \(y_2\) has no zeros in \((x_1,x_2)\), then \(y_1/y_2\) is either strictly increasing or strictly decreasing on \((x_1,x_2)\), and deduce a contradiction.
32.

33.

9.
(This exercise shows that if you know one nontrivial solution of \(\frac{d^2y}{dx^2}+p(x)\frac{dy}{dx}+q(x)y=0\), you can use Abel’s formula to find another.)
Suppose \(p\) and \(q\) are continuous and \(y_1\) is a solution of \[ \frac{d^2y}{dx^2}+p(x)\frac{dy}{dx}+q(x)y=0 {\tag A} \] that has no zeros on \((a,b)\). Let \(P(x)=\int p(x)\,dx\) be any antiderivative of \(p\) on \((a,b)\).
  1. Show that if \(K\) is an arbitrary nonzero constant and \(y_2\) satisfies \[ y_1\frac{dy_2}{dx}-\frac{dy_1}{dx}y_2=Ke^{-P(x)} {\tag B} \] on \((a,b)\), then \(y_2\) also satisfies (A) on \((a,b)\), and \(\{y_1,y_2\}\) is a fundamental set of solutions on (A) on \((a,b)\).
  2. Conclude from (a) that if \(y_2=uy_1\) where \(\frac{du}{dx}=K\displaystyle {e^{-P(x)}\over y_1^2(x)}\), then \(\{y_1,y_2\}\) is a fundamental set of solutions of (A) on \((a,b)\).

37.
Suppose \(p\) and \(q\) are continuous on \((a,b)\) and \(x_0\) is in \((a,b)\). Let \(y_1\) and \(y_2\) be the solutions of \[ \frac{d^2y}{dx^2}+p(x)\frac{dy}{dx}+q(x)y=0 {\tag A} \] such that \[ y_1(x_0)=1, \quad \frac{dy_1}{dx}(x_0)=0\quad \mbox{ and } \quad y_2(x_0)=0,\; \frac{dy_2}{dx}(x_0)=1\] (Theorem 5.1.1 implies that each of these IVPs has a unique solution on \((a,b)\).)
  1. Show that \(\{y_1,y_2\}\) is linearly independent on \((a,b)\).
  2. Show that an arbitrary solution \(y\) of (A) on \((a,b)\) can be written as \(y=y(x_0)y_1+\left.\frac{dy}{dx}\right|_{x_0}y_2\).
  3. Express the solution of the IVP \[ \frac{d^2y}{dx^2}+p(x)\frac{dy}{dx}+q(x)y=0,\quad y(x_0)=k_0,\quad \left.\frac{dy}{dx}\right|_{x_0}=k_1 \] as a linear combination of \(y_1\) and \(y_2\).

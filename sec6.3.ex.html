6.3 Exercises
Part 1: Free Oscillation
Write the equation of motion for a forced spring-mass system with damping. Convert each element of the equation into the correct variable for an RLC circuit using the Electrical and Mechanical Units Table. Substitute the values given to model the charge on the capacitor as a second order differential equation. Identify the initial condtitions and write an IVP to model the circuit. Identify the type of damping and find both the general solution and the particular solution of the IVP. Find the frequency, quasi-period, time-varying amplitude, phase angle, and then write amplitude-phase form of the particular solution. Finally graph both on one axis (they should match).
1.
An RLC circuit with a 3 \(\Omega \) resistor, a \(0.1\) H inductor, and 0.1 F capacitor is in free oscillation. If the initial inductor current is 2 amp and the initial charge is zero, determine and graph the charge as a function of time.
2.
An RLC circuit with a 2 \(\Omega \) resistor, a \(0.05\) H inductor, and 0.01 F capacitor is in free oscillation. If the initial inductor current is -2 amp and the initial charge is 2 C, determine and graph the charge as a function of time.
3.
An RLC circuit with a 2 \(\Omega \) resistor, a \(0.1\) H inductor, and 0.01 F capacitor is in free oscillation. If there is no initial inductor current and the initial charge is 2 C, determine and graph the charge as a function of time.
4.
An RLC circuit with a 6 \(\Omega \) resistor, a \(0.1\) H inductor, and 0.004 F capacitor is in free oscillation. If the initial inductor current is -10 amp and the initial charge is 3 C, determine and graph the charge as a function of time.
5.
An RLC circuit with a 4 \(\Omega \) resistor, a \(0.05\) H inductor, and 0.008 F capacitor is in free oscillation. If the initial inductor current is 2 amp and the initial charge is -1 C, determine and graph the charge as a function of time.

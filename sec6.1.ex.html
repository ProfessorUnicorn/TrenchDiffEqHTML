6.1 Exercises
Part 1: Undamped Free Motion
Use Hooke's Law \(mg=k\Delta l\) to set up the equation of motion \(m\ddot{y} + c\dot{y} + ky = 0 \) to model the displacement assuming that there’s no damping. Then set up an IVP to model the specific initial conditions. Find both the general solution and the particular solution to the IVP. Find the frequency, period, amplitude, and phase angle and then write amplitude-phase form of the solution. Finally graph both on one axis (they should match)
1.
An object stretches a spring 4 inches in equilibrium. Find and graph its displacement if it’s initially displaced 36 inches above equilibrium and given a downward velocity of 2 ft/s.
2.
An object stretches a string 1.2 inches in equilibrium. Find and graph its displacement if it’s initially displaced 3 inches below equilibrium and given a downward velocity of 2 ft/s.
3.
A spring with natural length .5 m has length 50.5 cm with a mass of 2 gm suspended from it. Find and graph its displacement if the mass is initially displaced 1.5 cm below equilibrium and released with zero velocity.
4.
An object stretches a spring 6 inches in equilibrium. Find and graph its displacement if it’s initially displaced 3 inches above equilibrium and given a downward velocity of 6 inches/s.
5.
An object stretches a spring 5 cm in equilibrium. Find and graph its displacement if it's initially displaced 10 cm above equilibrium and given an upward velocity of .25 m/s.
6.
A 10 kg mass stretches a spring 70 cm in equilibrium. Find and graph its displacement if a 2 kg mass is attached to the spring, initially displaced 25 cm below equilibrium, and given an upward velocity of 2 m/s.
7.
A weight stretches a spring 1.5 inches in equilibrium. Find and graph its displacement if the weight is initially displaced 8 inches above equilibrium and given a downward velocity of 4 ft/s.
8.
A weight stretches a spring 6 inches in equilibrium. Find and graph its displacement if the weight is initially displaced 6 inches above equilibrium and given a downward velocity of 3 ft/s.
Part 2: Undamped Forced Motion
Use Hooke's Law \(mg=k\Delta l\) to set up the equation of motion \(m\ddot{y} + c\dot{y} + ky = 0 \) to model the displacement assuming that there’s no damping. Then set up an IVP to model the specific initial conditions. Find both the general solution and the particular solution of the IVP. Then apply the initial conditions to obtain the constants. Finally convert the complementary solutions to amplitude-phase form by identifying the frequency and calculating the period, amplitude, and phase angle. Finally graph both forms on one axis (they should match)
10.
A 64 lb weight is attached to a spring with constant \(k=8\) lb/ft and subjected to an external force \(F(t)=2\sin t\). Find and graph its displacement if the weight is initially displaced 6 inches above equilibrium and given an upward velocity of 2 ft/s.
12.
A 4 lb weight stretches a spring 1 ft in equilibrium. Find and graph its displacement if an external force \(F(t)=(1/4)\sin 8 t\) lb is applied to the weight, which is initially displaced 4 inches above equilibrium and given a downward velocity of 1 ft/s.
13.
A 2 lb weight stretches a spring 6 inches in equilibrium. Find and graph its displacement if an external force \(F(t)=\sin 8t\) lb is applied to the weight, which is released from rest 2 inches below equilibrium.
15.
A 6 lb weight stretches a spring 6 inches in equilibrium. An external force \(F(t)=\displaystyle {3\over 16}\sin \omega t+\displaystyle {3\over 8}\cos \omega t \) lb is applied to the weight. For what value of \(\omega \) will the displacement be unbounded? Find and graph its displacement if \(\omega \) has this value. Assume that the motion starts from equilibrium with zero initial velocity.
16.
A 6 lb weight stretches a spring 4 inches in equilibrium. An external force \( F(t)=4\sin \omega t-6\cos \omega t \) lb is applied to the weight. For what value of \(\omega \) will the displacement be unbounded? Find and graph the displacement if \(\omega \) has this value. Assume that the motion starts from equilibrium with zero initial velocity.
17.
A mass of one kg is attached to a spring with constant \(k=4\) N/m. An external force \(F(t)=-\cos \omega t-2\sin \omega t\) n is applied to the mass. Find and graph its displacement if \(\omega \) equals the natural frequency of the spring–mass system. Assume that the mass is initially displaced 3 m above equilibrium and given an upward velocity of 450 cm/s.
Part 3: Challenge
11.
A unit mass hangs in equilibrium from a spring with constant \(k=1/16\). Find and graph its displacement if a force \(F(t)=3\sin t\) is applied to the mass.
9.
A spring–mass system has natural frequency \(7\sqrt {10}\) rad/s. The natural length of the spring is .7 m. What is the length of the spring when the mass is in equilibrium?
14.
A 10 gm mass suspended on a spring moves in simple harmonic motion with period 4 s. Find the period of the simple harmonic motion of a 20 gm mass suspended from the same spring.
18.
An object is in simple harmonic motion with frequency \(\omega _0\), with \(y(0)=y_0\) and \(\left. \dot{y} \right| _{0}=v_0\). Find its displacement for \(t>0\). Also, find the amplitude of the oscillation and give formulas for the sine and cosine of the initial phase angle.
19.
Two objects suspended from identical springs are set into motion. The period of one object is twice the period of the other. How are the weights of the two objects related?
20.
Two objects suspended from identical springs are set into motion. The weight of one object is twice the weight of the other. How are the periods of the resulting motions related?
21.
Two identical objects suspended from different springs are set into motion. The period of one motion is 3 times the period of the other. How are the two spring constants related?

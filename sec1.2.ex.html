1.
Find the order of the equation.
  1. \(\displaystyle {d^2y\over dx^2}+2 {dy\over dx}\ {d^3y\over dx^3}+x=0\)
  2. \(\displaystyle\frac{d^2y}{dx^2}-3\frac{dy}{dx}+2y=x^7\)
  3. \(\displaystyle\frac{dy}{dx}-y^7=0\)
  4. \(\displaystyle\frac{d^2y}{dx^2}y-\left(\frac{dy}{dx}\right)^2=2\)
2.

Verify that the function is a solution of the differential equation on some interval, for any choice of the arbitrary constants appearing in the function.

  1. \(y=ce^{2x}; \quad \displaystyle\frac{dy}{dx}=2y\)
  2. \(\displaystyle y= {x^2\over 3} +{c\over x}; \quad x\frac{dy}{dx}+y=x^2\)
  3. \(\displaystyle y= {1\over 2}+ce^{-x^2}; \quad \frac{dy}{dx}+2xy=x\)
  4. \(\displaystyle y=(1+ce^{-x^2/2}); (1-ce^{-x^2/2})^{-1} \quad 2\frac{dy}{dx}+x(y^2-1)=0\)
  5. \(\displaystyle y={\tan \left ( {x^3\over 3}+c\right )}; \quad \frac{dy}{dx}=x^2(1+y^2)\)
  6. \(\displaystyle y=(c_1+c_2x)e^x+\sin x+x^2; \quad \frac{d^2y}{dx^2}-2\frac{dy}{dx}+y=-2 \cos x+x^2-4x+2\)
  7. \(\displaystyle y=c_1e^x+c_2x+ {2\over x}; \quad (1-x)\frac{d^2y}{dx^2}+x\frac{dy}{dx}- y=4(1-x-x^2)x^{-3}\)
  8. \(y=x^{-1/2}(c_1\sin x+c_2 \cos x)+4x+8 \)
    \(\displaystyle x^2\frac{d^2y}{dx^2}+x\frac{dy}{dx}+ {\left (x^2-{1\over 4}\right )}y=4x^3+8x^2+3x-2\)
3.

Find all solutions of the equation.

  1. \(\displaystyle\frac{dy}{dx}=-x\)
  2. \(\displaystyle\frac{dy}{dx}=-x \sin x\)
  3. \(\displaystyle\frac{dy}{dx}=x \ln x\)
  4. \(\displaystyle\frac{d^2y}{dx^2}=x \cos x\)
  5. \(\displaystyle\frac{d^2y}{dx^2}=2xe^x\)
  6. \(\displaystyle\frac{d^2y}{dx^2}=2x+\sin x+e^x\)
  7. \(\displaystyle\frac{d^3y}{dx^3} =-\cos x\)
  8. \(\displaystyle\frac{d^3y}{dx^3}=-x^2+e^x\)
  9. \(\displaystyle\frac{d^3y}{dx^3}=7e^{4x}\)
4.

Solve the initial value problem.

  1. \(\displaystyle\frac{dy}{dx}=-xe^x, \quad y(0)=1\)
  2. \(\displaystyle {\frac{dy}{dx}=x \sin x^2, \quad y\left ({\sqrt {\pi \over 2}}\right )=1}\)
  3. \(\displaystyle\frac{dy}{dx}=\tan x, \quad y(\pi /4)=3\)
  4. \(\displaystyle\frac{d^2y}{dx^2}=x^4, \quad y(2)=-1, \quad \left.\frac{dy}{dx}\right|_2=-1\)
  5. \(\displaystyle\frac{d^2y}{dx^2}=xe^{2x}, \quad y(0)=7, \quad \left.\frac{dy}{dx}\right|_0=1\)
  6. \(\displaystyle\frac{d^2y}{dx^2}=- x \sin x, \quad y(0)=1, \quad \left.\frac{dy}{dx}\right|_0=-3\)
  7. \(\displaystyle\frac{d^3y}{dx^3}=x^2e^x, \quad y(0)=1, \quad \left.\frac{dy}{dx}\right|_0=-2, \quad \left.\frac{d^2y}{dx^2}\right|_0=3\)
  8. \(\displaystyle\frac{d^3y}{dx^3}=2+\sin 2x, \quad y(0)=1, \quad \left.\frac{dy}{dx}\right|_0=-6, \quad \left.\frac{d^2y}{dx^2}\right|_0=3\)
  9. \(\displaystyle\frac{d^3y}{dx^3}=2x+1, \quad y(2)=1, \quad \left.\frac{dy}{dx}\right|_2=-4, \quad \left.\frac{d^2y}{dx^2}\right|_2=7\)
5.

Verify that the function is a solution of the initial value problem.

  1. \(\displaystyle y=x\cos x; \quad \frac{dy}{dx}=\cos x-y\tan x, \quad y(\pi /4)=\displaystyle {\pi \over 4\sqrt {2}}\)
  2. \(\displaystyle {y={1+2\ln x\over x^2}+{1\over 2}; \quad \frac{dy}{dx}={x^2-2x^2y+2\over x^3}, \quad y(1)={3\over 2}}\)
  3. \(\displaystyle y={\tan \left ({x^2\over 2}\right )}; \quad \frac{dy}{dx}=x(1+y^2), \quad y(0)=0\)
  4. \(\displaystyle {y={2\over x-2}; \quad \frac{dy}{dx}={-y(y+1)\over x}}, \quad y(1)=-2\)
6.

  1. \(\displaystyle y=x^2(1+\ln x); \quad \frac{d^2y}{dx^2}= {3x\frac{dy}{dx}-4y\over x^2}, \quad y(e)=2e^2, \quad \left.\frac{dy}{dx}\right|_e=5e\)
  2. \(\displaystyle y={x^2\over 3}+x-1; \quad \frac{d^2y}{dx^2}= {x^2-x\frac{dy}{dx}+y+1\over x^2}, \quad y(1)= {1\over 3}, \quad \left.\frac{dy}{dx}\right|_1= {5\over 3}\)
  3. \(\displaystyle y=(1+x^2)^{-1/2}; \quad \frac{d^2y}{dx^2}= {(x^2-1)y-x(x^2+1)\frac{dy}{dx}\over (x^2+1)^2}, \quad y(0)=1,\)
  4. \(\displaystyle y={x^2\over 1-x}; \quad \frac{d^2y}{dx^2}= {2(x+y)(x\frac{dy}{dx}-y)\over x^3}, \quad y(1/2)=1/2, \quad \left.\frac{dy}{dx}\right|_{1/2}=3\)
7.

Suppose an object is launched from a point 320 feet above the earth with an initial velocity of 128 ft/sec upward, and the only force acting on it thereafter is gravity. Take \(g=32\) ft/sec\(^2\).

  1. Find the highest altitude attained by the object.
  2. Determine how long it takes for the object to fall to the ground.
8.

Let \(a\) be a nonzero real number.

  1. Verify that if \(c\) is an arbitrary constant then \[ y=(x-c)^a \, {\rm (A)} \] is a solution of \[ \displaystyle\frac{dy}{dx}=ay^{(a-1)/a} \, {\rm (B)} \] on \((c,\infty )\).
  2. Suppose \(a<0\) or \(a>1\). Can you think of a solution of (B) that isn’t of the form (A)?
9.

  1. Verify that if \(c\) is any real number then \[ y=c^2+cx+2c+1 \, {\rm (A)} \] satisfies \[ \displaystyle\frac{dy}{dx}={-(x+2)+\sqrt {x^2+4x+4y}\over 2} \, {\rm (B)} \] on some open interval. Identify the open interval.

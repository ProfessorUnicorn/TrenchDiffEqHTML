0.4 Calculus I
Limits
Basic Limits \begin{align*} \lim_{x \to a} c = & c \\ \lim_{x \to a} x = & a \\ \end{align*} Limit Laws \begin{align*} \lim_{x \to a} \Big( c \cdot f(x) \Big) = & c \cdot \lim_{x \to a} f(x) \\ \lim_{x \to a} \Big( f(x) + g(x) \Big) = & \lim_{x \to a} f(x) + \lim_{x \to a} g(x) \\ \lim_{x \to a} \Big( f(x) \cdot g(x) \Big) = & \lim_{x \to a} f(x) \cdot \lim_{x \to a} g(x) \\ \lim_{x \to a} \left( \frac{ f(x) } { g(x) } \right) = & \frac{ \displaystyle \lim_{x \to a} f(x) }{ \displaystyle \lim_{x \to a} g(x) } \\ \lim_{x \to a} \Big( f^n(x) \Big) = & \Big( \lim_{x \to a} f(x) \Big)^n \\ \end{align*} Special Limits \begin{align*} \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin x}{x} = & 1 \\ \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{1-\cos x}{x} = & 0 \\ \end{align*} Infinite Limits \begin{align*} \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{1}{x} = & 0 \\ \lim_{x \to 0^+} \frac{1}{x} = & \infty \\ \lim_{x \to 0^-} \frac{1}{x} = & -\infty \\ \lim_{x \to \infty} e^x = & \infty \\ \lim_{x \to -\infty} e^x = & 0 \\ \end{align*}
Derivatives
Limit Definition \begin{equation*} f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h} \end{equation*} Basic Derivative Rules \begin{align*} \frac{d}{dx} \Big( c \Big) = & 0 \\ \frac{d}{dx} \Big( x \Big) = & 1 \\ \frac{d}{dx} \Big( c \cdot f(x) \Big) = & c \cdot f'(x) \\ \frac{d}{dx} \Big( f(x) + g(x) \Big) = & f'(x) + g'(x) \\ \end{align*}
Use the limit definition to prove the basic derivative rules
Advanced Derivative Rules \begin{align*} \frac{d}{dx} \Big( x^n \Big) = & nx^{n-1} \\ \frac{d}{dx} \Big( f(x) \cdot g(x) \Big) = & f(x) \cdot g'(x) + g(x) \cdot f'(x) \\ \frac{d}{dx} \left( \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} \right) = & \frac{g(x) \cdot f'(x) - f(x) \cdot g'(x)}{ g^2(x) } \\ \frac{d}{dx} \bigg( f \Big( g(x) \Big) \bigg) = & f' \Big( g(x) \Big) \cdot g'(x) \\ \end{align*}
Use the power rule to calculate \( \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \sqrt[n]{x} \Big) \) and \( \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \frac{1}{x^n} \Big) \)
Use the power and chain rule to calculate \( \frac{d}{dx} \Big( f^n(x) \Big) \)
Trig Derivative Rules \begin{align*} \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \sin x \Big) = & \cos x \\ \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \cos x \Big) = & -\sin x \\ \end{align*}
Use the limit definition to prove the sine and cosine derivative rules
Use the quotient rule to calculate the rest of the trig derivatives: \( \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \tan x \Big) \), \( \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \sec x \Big) \), \( \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \cot x \Big) \), and \( \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \csc x \Big) \)
Use the power and chain rule to calculate \( \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \sin^n x \Big) \), \( \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \sin f(x) \Big) \), \( \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \sin^n f(x) \Big) \), and \( \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \sin f^n(x) \Big) \)
Inverse Trig Derivatives \begin{align*} \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \sin^{-1} x \Big) = & \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}} \\ \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \cos^{-1} x \Big) = & -\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}} \\ \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \tan^{-1} x \Big) = & \frac{1}{1+x^2} \\ \end{align*}
Use implicit differentiation to calculate the derivatives of the inverse trig functions: \( \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \sin^{-1} x \Big) \), \( \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \cos^{-1} x \Big) \), and \( \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \tan^{-1} x \Big) \)
Log and Exponential Derivative Rules \begin{align*} \frac{d}{dx} \Big( e^x \Big) = & e^x \\ \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \ln x \Big) = & \frac{1}{x} \\ \end{align*}
Use implicit differentiation to prove \( \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \ln x \Big) = \frac{1}{x} \)
Use implicit differentiation to prove \( \frac{d}{dx} \Big( b^{x} \Big) = b^x \ln b \)
Use the chain rule to calculate \( \frac{d}{dx} \Big( e^{f(x)} \Big) \) and \( \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \ln f(x) \Big) \)
Use the change of base formula to prove \( \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \log_b{x} \Big) = \frac{1}{x \ln b} \)

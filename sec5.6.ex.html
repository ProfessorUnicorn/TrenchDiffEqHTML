Part 1: Nonhomogeneous
Verify \( \displaystyle y_1\) satisfies the complementary equation then use reduction of order to find a first order equation in \( \displaystyle {du}/{dx}.\) Substitute \( \displaystyle z = {du}/{dx} \) to rewrite the equation then solve for \( \displaystyle z\) and finally substitute back to find \( \displaystyle u\) then the general solution \( \displaystyle y\). Finally, find a fundamental set of solutions of the complementary equation.

2.

15.

16.

Part 3: IVPs
Solve the IVP
31.
\( \displaystyle \displaystyle x^2 \frac{d^2y}{dx^2} -3x \frac{dy}{dx} +4y=4x^4,\quad y(-1)=7,\quad \left. \frac{dy}{dx} \right|_{-1}=-8; \quad y_1=x^2\)
32.
\( \displaystyle (3x-1) \frac{d^2y}{dx^2} -(3x+2) \frac{dy}{dx} -(6x-8)y=0, \quad y(0)=2,\; \left. \frac{dy}{dx} \right|_{0}=3; \quad y_1=e^{2x}\)
33.
\( \displaystyle (x+1)^2 \frac{d^2y}{dx^2} -2(x+1) \frac{dy}{dx} -(x^2+2x-1)y=(x+1)^3e^x, \quad y(0)=1,\quad \left. \frac{dy}{dx} \right|_{0}=~-1\);
\( \displaystyle y_1=(x+1)e^x\)
34.

Part 5: Challenge
55.
A generalized Riccati equation is of the form \[ \frac{dy}{dx} = P(x)+Q(x)y+R(x)y^2 {\tag A} \] (If \(R\equiv -1\), (A) is a Riccati equation.) Let \(y_1\) be a known solution and \(y\) an arbitrary solution of (A). Let \(z=y-y_1\). Show that \(z\) is a solution of a Bernoulli equation with \(n=2\).
Given that \(y_1\) is a solution of the given equation, use the method suggested by Exercise 55 to find other solutions.

36.
Suppose \( \displaystyle p_1\) and \( \displaystyle p_2\) are continuous on \( \displaystyle (a,b)\). Let \( \displaystyle y_1\) be a solution of \[ \frac{d^2y}{dx^2} +p_1(x) \frac{dy}{dx} +p_2(x)y=0 {\tag A} \] that has no zeros on \( \displaystyle (a,b)\), and let \( \displaystyle x_0\) be in \( \displaystyle (a,b)\). Use reduction of order to show that \( \displaystyle y_1\) and \[ y_2(x)=y_1(x)\int ^x_{x_0}{1\over y^2_1(t)} \exp \left (-\int ^t_{x_0}p_1(s)\, ds\right )\,dt \] form a fundamental set of solutions of (A) on \( \displaystyle (a,b)\). (Note: This exercise is related to Exercise 9.)
37.

  1. Show that \( \displaystyle y\) is a solution of (A) if and only if \( \displaystyle y={ \frac{dz}{dx} /z}\), where \[ \frac{d^2z}{dx^2} +p(x) \frac{dz}{dx} +q(x)z=0. {\tag B} \]

40.
The nonlinear first order equation \[ \frac{dy}{dx} +r(x)y^2+p(x)y+q(x)=0 \, {\tag A} \] is the generalized Riccati equation. (See Exercise 2.4. 55.) Assume that \( \displaystyle p\) and \( \displaystyle q\) are continuous and \( \displaystyle r\) is differentiable.
  1. Show that \( y\) is a solution of (A) if and only if \( \displaystyle y= \frac{dz}{dx} \frac{1}{rz}\), where \[ \frac{d^2z}{dx^2} +\left [p(x)- \frac{dr}{dx} \frac{1}{r(x)} \right ] \frac{dz}{dx} +r(x)q(x)z=0. {\tag B} \]
  2. Show that the general solution of (A) is \[ y={c_1 \frac{dz_1}{dx} +c_2 \frac{dz_2}{dx} \over r(c_1z_1+c_2z_2)}\] where \( \displaystyle \{z_1,z_2\}\) is a fundamental set of solutions of (B) and \( \displaystyle c_1\) and \( \displaystyle c_2\) are arbitrary constants.

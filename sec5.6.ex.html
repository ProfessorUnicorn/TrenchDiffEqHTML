In Exercises 1–17 find the general solution, given that \(y_1\) satisfies the complementary equation. As a byproduct, find a fundamental set of solutions of the complementary equation.

36.

Suppose \(p_1\) and \(p_2\) are continuous on \((a,b)\). Let \(y_1\) be a solution of \[ y''+p_1(x)y'+p_2(x)y=0 \, {\rm (A)} \] that has no zeros on \((a,b)\), and let \(x_0\) be in \((a,b)\). Use reduction of order to show that \(y_1\) and \[ y_2(x)=y_1(x)\int ^x_{x_0}{1\over y^2_1(t)} \exp \left (-\int ^t_{x_0}p_1(s)\, ds\right )\,dt \] form a fundamental set of solutions of (A) on \((a,b)\). (NOTE: This exercise is related to Exercise 9.)

37.

The nonlinear first order equation \[ y'+y^2+p(x)y+q(x)=0 \, {\rm (A)} \] is a Riccati equation. (See Exercise 2.4. 55.) Assume that \(p\) and \(q\) are continuous.

(a)

Show that \(y\) is a solution of (A) if and only if \(y={z'/z}\), where \[ z''+p(x)z'+q(x)z=0. \, {\rm (B)} \]

(b)

Show that the general solution of (A) is \[ y={c_1z'_1+c_2z'_2\over c_1z_1+c_2z_2}, \, {\rm (C)} \] where \(\{z_1,z_2\}\) is a fundamental set of solutions of (B) and \(c_1\) and \(c_2\) are arbitrary constants.

(c)

Does the formula (C) imply that the first order equation (A) has a two–parameter family of solutions? Explain your answer.

(a)

(b)
